Pook's Pick


Hit or Shit

Star Wars : The Force Awakens

Star Wars: The Force Awakens is Disney’s new tentpole property. After basically rushing it out, how well does it hold up?
Well, a lot better than it should, but still not all that well. Let me explain.
The movie itself isn’t bad, but much like its main antagonist (played so-so-ly by Adam Driver), it is living in the shadow of huge legacy that it just can’t imitate. Instead of going on the unexpected route and making something completely original, Disney opts to play it safe and basically rehash the OT. In my showing, this went really well: people clapped and cheered as characters appeared, lines were uttered and certain things happened, reminding them of their childhood (or newly found) love of the OT. So why is this a bad thing? Let’s say you see a magician and he does a magic trick, it’s amazing, and you shit your pants. Now imagine he does it again with new props. It’s still good, but it’s lost a lot of the charm, right? That’s what we have here: mostly new props but a lot of the same tricks. Now that all that is out of the way, you might be asking “If that’s his one complaint then why is it a shit rating?” It feels soulless. It feels like a tentpole project. Some of the new characters show promise but desperately need developing. All the cues and plot beats from the same trilogy are here, nothing is a surprise. The acting is just fine, the story is serviceable (or more so if you’re a fanboy), the CGI is good, the battles are ok, the swordplay is miles better than the prequels (no more kung fu sabres, thank god), but it all basically is, that’s it. The Force Awakens is nothing more than a rehash of the OT, but it also offers a promising start to what could be a good trilogy of movies, if Disney will allow the filmmakers to step out of it’s predecessors shadows and forge its own path. If you go in a fan of Star Wars, you’ll love it. If you go in any other way, you’ll think it is perfectly average. Highlights: The new characters are good and mostly interesting. The action scenes are good. Somewhat compelling start. Lowlights: Too much pandering to the fans, story is rehashed.
